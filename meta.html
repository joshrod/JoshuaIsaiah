Overview

It's very important for emerging front-end developers to have their own personal space so they can showcase their skills and portfolio work.

The goal of this site is to provide potential future employers and clients with the insight necessary to see if my skills can match the requirements they are looking for.

Website Details

The front page serves as the overview that I wanted to provide. From this page, the user can tell who I am, what I do, and how to get in touch with me. The site goes further into detail as the user advances through the navigation links.

The user can also get a sense of what kind of design I favor: simple and minimalistic. I find that information is easily related this way, especially now. The black and white colors help give a sense of professionalism to the site, while the colored items of the page show the separation of content and design.

The site is only as large as the user needs it to be. Casual browsers can see a four-page summary, while employers and clients can go in-depth into my portfolio and work experience.

The important part is that this amount of detail is not forced upon. This was intended to provide the best experience possible for likely viewers.

epilogue
